India: General Introduction

1. Geographical Overview

  • India is the 7th largest country by geographical area and the 1st largest by population.
  • It occupies approximately 2.4% of the world's total land area (lithosphere).
  • The total land boundary stretches for 15,200 km, while the coastline boundary covers 7,516.6 km.
  • India's national population density stands at 382 persons per sq. km.

2. Extreme Points of India

  • Northernmost: Indira Col, located near the Siachen Glacier (J&K).
  • Southernmost (Mainland): Kanyakumari, Tamil Nadu.
  • Southernmost (Absolute): Indira Point or Pygmalion Point.
  • Westernmost: Guhar Moti (Gujarat).
  • Easternmost: Kibithu (Arunachal Pradesh).

3. International Boundaries & Relations

Boundary Lines

  • Radcliffe Line: India and Pakistan.
  • Durand Line: India and Afghanistan.
  • McMahon Line: India and China.
  • Palk Strait: India and Sri Lanka.

Boundary Stats

  • Largest Land Boundary: Bangladesh (4,096.7 km).
  • Shortest Land Boundary: Afghanistan (106 km).

4. State-International Borders

  • Pakistan: Ladakh, J&K, Punjab, Rajasthan, Gujarat.
  • Afghanistan: Ladakh.
  • China: Ladakh, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh.
  • Nepal: Uttarakhand, UP, Bihar, West Bengal, Sikkim.
  • Bhutan: Sikkim, West Bengal, Assam, Arunachal Pradesh.
  • Myanmar: Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram.
  • Sri Lanka: Coastline boundary with Tamil Nadu.
