1. Introduction to Number System

A Number System is a method of representing numbers using digits and mathematical symbols. Every number belongs to a particular category depending upon its properties. The Number System forms the foundation of Algebra, Arithmetic and Higher Mathematics. Questions from this topic are frequently asked in the NDA Examination.

2. Classification of Number System

The Number System is broadly classified into Real Numbers and Imaginary Numbers. Real Numbers are further divided into Rational and Irrational Numbers.

Numbers

├── Real Numbers (R)
│    ├── Rational Numbers (Q)
│    │    ├── Integers (Z)
│    │    │    ├── Negative Integers
│    │    │    ├── Zero
│    │    │    └── Positive Integers
│    │            └── Natural Numbers
│    │
│    └── Irrational Numbers
└── Imaginary Numbers (i)
🎯 NDA Tip Remember this flowchart carefully. Every year NDA asks direct or indirect questions based on the classification of numbers.

3. Real Numbers

All numbers that can be represented on the Number Line are called Real Numbers.

Real Numbers = Rational Numbers + Irrational Numbers
Real Numbers Examples
Integers -5, -2, 0, 4, 15
Fractions 2/3, 7/9, -5/8
Decimals 3.25, 0.75, 8.125
Irrational √2, √3, π

4. Imaginary Numbers

Numbers involving the square root of a negative number are called Imaginary Numbers.

√(-1) = i

√(-4) = 2i

√(-9) = 3i

√(-25) = 5i
Expression Value
√(-1) i
√(-16) 4i
√(-49) 7i
√(-100) 10i

5. Important Formula

√ab = √a × √b

√(a²) = |a|

i² = -1

i⁴ = 1

Natural Numbers are the counting numbers used in our daily life. They start from 1 and continue infinitely. Zero is not included in Natural Numbers.

N = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, ...∞}

Includes Zero? ❌ No
Examples 1,2,3,4,5...

7. Whole Numbers (W)

Whole Numbers are obtained by adding Zero to the set of Natural Numbers.

W = {0,1,2,3,4,5,6...∞}
Property Details
Smallest Whole Number 0
Largest Whole Number Does not exist
Contains Zero? ✔ Yes

Z = {......,-3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3......}

Solved Example 1

Question: Which of the following is a Whole Number but not a Natural Number?

  • A) 5
  • B) 2
  • C) 0
  • D) 9
Solution: Whole Numbers = {0,1,2,3...} Natural Numbers = {1,2,3...} Therefore, Answer = 0 (Option C)

9. Rational Numbers (Q)

A Rational Number is any number that can be written in the form p/q, where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0.

Rational Number = p / q

Where, p = Integer
q = Integer (q ≠ 0)
Rational Numbers Examples
Positive Fraction 3/5
Negative Fraction -7/9
Integer 5 = 5/1
Terminating Decimal 0.25 = 25/100
Recurring Decimal 0.333... = 1/3
🎯 NDA Tip Every Integer is a Rational Number, but every Rational Number is not an Integer.

10. Irrational Numbers

Numbers which cannot be expressed in p/q form are called Irrational Numbers. They have non-terminating and non-recurring decimal expansion.

Examples

√2
√3
√5
π
√7
Rational Numbers Irrational Numbers
Can be written as p/q Cannot be written as p/q
Decimals terminate or repeat Decimals never terminate or repeat
2/5, 4, -9 √2, √5, π

Solved Example 2

Question: Which of the following is an Irrational Number?

  • A) 7/9
  • B) √16
  • C) √3
  • D) -8
Solution

√16 = 4 (Rational)
7/9 is Rational
-8 is Rational

Only √3 is Irrational.

Answer : Option C

11. Prime Numbers

A Prime Number has exactly two factors: 1 and the number itself.

Prime Numbers

Property Prime Number
Factors Exactly 2
Smallest Prime Number 2
Only Even Prime Number 2

12. Composite Numbers

A Composite Number has more than two factors.

Composite Numbers

4,6,8,9,10,12,14,15,16...
Prime Number Composite Number
Exactly 2 factors More than 2 factors
2,3,5,7,11... 4,6,8,9,10...
💡 Important Facts
  • 1 is neither Prime nor Composite.
  • 2 is the only Even Prime Number.
  • Every Composite Number has more than two factors.

13. Even Numbers

An Even Number is any integer that is exactly divisible by 2. Its remainder is always 0.

Even Number = 2n

where n = 0,1,2,3,...
Property Even Numbers
Divisible by 2
Last Digit 0, 2, 4, 6, 8
Examples 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12...

14. Odd Numbers

An Odd Number is an integer which is not divisible by 2.

Odd Number = 2n + 1
Property Odd Numbers

Last Digit 1,3,5,7,9
Examples 1,3,5,7,9,11...

16. Divisibility Rules

Number Rule
2 Last digit is even.
3 Sum of digits is divisible by 3.
4 Last two digits divisible by 4.
5 Ends with 0 or 5.
6 Divisible by both 2 and 3.
8 Last three digits divisible by 8.
9 Sum of digits divisible by 9.
10 Ends with 0.
11 Difference of alternate digit sums is 0 or divisible by 11.
🎯 NDA Tip Questions on divisibility rules are frequently used in HCF, LCM and simplification problems.

Solved Example 3

Question: Is 792 divisible by 9?

Solution 7 + 9 + 2 = 18 18 is divisible by 9. Therefore, 792 is divisible by 9.

17. Highest Common Factor (HCF)

The Highest Common Factor (HCF) is the greatest number that divides two or more numbers exactly. It is also called the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD).

HCF = Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)

Example

Find the HCF of 12 and 18.

Factors of 12 = 1,2,3,4,6,12
Factors of 18 = 1,2,3,6,9,18

Common Factors = 1,2,3,6

HCF = 6

18. Least Common Multiple (LCM)

The Least Common Multiple (LCM) is the smallest positive number that is exactly divisible by two or more numbers.

LCM = Smallest Common Multiple

Example

Find the LCM of 12 and 18.

Multiples of 12 12,24,36,48...

Multiples of 18 18,36,54...

LCM = 36

Important Formula

HCF × LCM = Product of the Numbers
🎯 NDA Tip If two numbers are co-prime, their HCF = 1.

Solved Example 4

Question: Find the HCF and LCM of 8 and 12.

Factors of 8 = 1,2,4,8
Factors of 12 = 1,2,3,4,6,12

HCF = 4

LCM = 24
